Motivation

People increasingly concern about keeping fit and healthy eating habits


Mainstream diet record apps require tedious operations


Current food recognition systems focus on single dish recognition and simple western food

Objective


To build a food recognition and nutrition estimating system, which can


1.

Detect and recognize bowls and plates in one image in low latency

2.

Recognize Chinese food with good performance by integrating multi information

3.

Estimate calorie and nutrition data in good accuracy

System Design


Multi-dish recognition
Detect each dishes by using YOLO-V3 before classification

Multi-task learning
Fine-tune ResNet50 to a multi-task learning model for better classification

Real-time Streaming, Detection, and Classification
Utilize Kafka and Spark Streaming to enable real time detecting and classification

YOLOv3 is a very powerful detector. If use Chinese food data set directly to train an object detection model, the results could not be good. Because Chinese foods are always served on a plate or bowl, we finally decided to use YOLOv3 model that trained on COCO to detect dishware in food images.

Object Detection

In our project, we proposed a multi-task learning method utilizing cuisine method recognition as an auxiliary task to gain better accuracy of food classification.

Dataset: VireoFood-172, contains 110,241 food images from 172 typical Chinese food categories and annotated manually according to 353 ingredients.

Image Classification

we assume that the 3D shape of food is related to its category, and use the estimated size of food from the top view image to calculate the volume of food items.

After the detection of objects with bounding boxes, we apply grabcut on each bbox to obtain an accurate food region.

Volume Estimation


In our system, we use spark streaming to receive users’ images via Kafka and process them by RDD operations using spark. After processing, we send the results data back to users via Kafka, and also store the result data in Cassandra.
