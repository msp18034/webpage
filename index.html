Image-based Dietary Assessment System in the Cloud

Group: Yang Xueya & Zhang Zixuan (Professor C.L. Wang)

Our aim is to develop an application that can detect and recognize multi Chinese dishes from a image and estimate their nutrition data as quickly and accurately as possible.

Motivation

With the growing concern about keeping fit and healthy eating habits, the need to accurately measure personal daily diet becomes imperative.

The mainstream diet record apps need users to manually enter the name of each dish per meal per day and then record the corresponding calories and nutrition data after searching.

Automatic food identification and analysis is a challenging task because dishes are highly variable in appearance and color due to the cooking method and ingredient location, especially for Chinese food.

Although there are many research teams aiming to develop Chinese food recognition system, they mainly focus on single dish recognition, which is not practical enough in real life.

Objective

To build a food recognition and nutrition estimating system, which can

1.

Recognize multi-dishes in one picture

2.

Recognize Chinese food with good performance by integrating content(ingredients) information

3.

Estimate calorie and nutrition data in good accuracy

System Design

Use an object detection network to extract bounding boxes of food dishes from a multi-dishes photo and obtain a cropped image corresponding to each bounding box which contains only one dish.
Provide the cropped dish images to the category recognition network and use Grabcut to segment food and reference(student card) from image to calculate volume one by one.
The calories of the cropped dishes will be calculated based on the calculated volume and the corresponding data of the adjusted category in food and nutrition database.
All fine-tuned types and intake of nutrient data will be saved and exported to the user’s phone, and diet suggestions will also be given on the basis of simple rules.

The object detection algorithms complete the input from the original image to the output of object position and category. In practice, we will try the Faster RCNN (fine-tune the pre-trained layers like VGG-16, Res-net) and YOLO (YOLOv3 and YOLOv3-tiny) to find the algorithm that have best performace related to our issue.

Dataset: UEC-FOOD-256, contains 256-kind food photos and each food photo has a bounding box indicating the location of the food items in the photo. This dataset includes more than 100 single-label images for each category, we further screened 112 types of foods that are common in China, to make training more practical.

Object Detection

In image classification, we will use ingredients label to further adjusted the category of cropped dish images. We will try the popular classification algorithms ( including VGGNet, Inception, ResNet and Xception) run them on the cloud. Finally, the algorithm with the best performance in terms of time and accuracy will be selected.

Dataset:VireoFood-172, contains 110,241 food images from 172 typical Chinese food categories and annotated manually according to 353 ingredients. The ingredient label for each image is represented as a vector, each element in the vector denotes the occurrence of the ingredient.

After the detection of objects with bounding boxes, we apply grabcut on each bbox to obtain an accurate food region.

To make the training and testing faster, we deploy the whole project on the cloud: the provided Kubernetes cluster with at least 16 Docker containers, of which each has 8G memory and 2 vCPUs. We would deploy Hadoop and Spark and prepare other necessary environments on this k8s cluster.

During the survey and previous study in COMP7305, we have found several distributed frameworks of Tensorflow, Keras on spark. These open-source project could help us adapt our single-node network to the spark cluster.
